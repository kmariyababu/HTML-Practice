Egg omlet

Ingredients:

  • Eggs (2-4, depending on your preference)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Onions (3-4)
  • turmeric powder 1/2 table spoons
  • Salt as required for taste
  • Chilli Powder
  • Butter or cooking oil (1-2 teaspoons)
  • Optional fillings: diced vegetables, cheese, ham, herbs, etc.

Equipment:

  • Mixing bowl
  • Whisk or fork
  • Non-stick frying pan
  • Spatula

Instructions:

    prepare the eggs

  1. Crack the eggs into a mixing bowl
  2. Add a pinch of salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Step 3: Instruction 3
    4. Prepare Ingredients:
  4. If you're using any additional fillings like vegetables or cheese, chop or grate them and set aside.

    5. Whisk Eggs:

  5. Whisk the eggs using a fork or a whisk until the yolks and whites are well combined. You can add a splash of milk or cream if you like for a fluffier texture, although it's optional.

    6. Preheat Pan:

  6. Place a non-stick frying pan over medium heat.
  7. Add butter or cooking oil to coat the pan evenly. Allow it to melt and become hot but not smoking.

    8. Pour Eggs into Pan:

  8. Once the butter or oil is hot, pour the whisked eggs into the pan.

    9. Swirl and Cook:

  9. Swirl the pan to spread the eggs evenly.
  10. Allow the eggs to cook undisturbed for a minute or until the edges start to set.

    11. Add Fillings (Optional):

  11. If you're adding fillings, sprinkle them over one half of the omelet.

    12. Fold and Flip:

  12. Once the edges of the omelet are set but the center is still slightly runny, use a spatula to fold the omelet in half, covering the fillings if any.
  13. You can also flip the omelet carefully if you prefer a more evenly cooked texture.

    14. Finish Cooking:

  14. Allow the omelet to cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until the center is fully cooked but still moist.
  15. Cooking time may vary based on your preference for doneness.

    16. Serve:

  16. Slide the omelet onto a plate.
  17. Garnish with herbs or additional toppings if desired.
  18. Remember, making the perfect omelet may take a bit of practice, so feel free to experiment with.
